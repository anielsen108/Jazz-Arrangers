# Ryan Truesdell (b. 1973)

## Biography

Ryan Truesdell was born in Reno, Nevada, and became an arranger, conductor, and scholar dedicated to realizing Gil Evans's unrealized arrangements. He studied at Cincinnati Conservatory and Eastman School of Music before establishing himself in New York. Truesdell founded the Gil Evans Project to perform Evans's previously unrecorded arrangements, working from original scores in Evans's archive. His albums "Lines of Color" and "Centennial" won Grammy Awards and widespread critical acclaim. Beyond Evans's work, Truesdell creates his own arrangements and conducts various ensembles. His dedication demonstrates deep understanding of Evans's aesthetic while bringing fresh scholarly and practical perspectives. Truesdell represents best of contemporary jazz arranging scholarship and practice, showing how honoring tradition and creating new work aren't contradictory activities but complementary aspects of keeping music vital.

## Musical Style

Truesdell's work with Gil Evans's materials demonstrates deep understanding of Evans's orchestral approach—careful attention to color, sophisticated harmonies, and organic development. His own arranging style draws from Evans while developing personal voice. What distinguishes Truesdell's work is its scholarly rigor combined with musical vitality—his realizations of Evans's scores are historically informed yet make Evans's vision live for contemporary audiences. His conducting and preparation bring Evans's complex scores to life with clarity and emotional impact. Truesdell's harmonic language and orchestral thinking draw from Evans's influence while incorporating contemporary perspectives. His style represents serious engagement with jazz tradition: respectful, knowledgeable, and demonstrating that historical work and creative practice enrich each other.
