## 1920s-1930s: The Big Band Era Begins

This era saw the birth of jazz arranging as a distinct art form, with arrangers creating the frameworks that would define big band jazz.

1. [Don Redman](arrangers/1920s-1930s/don-redman.md) - Pioneer of big band arranging
2. [Fletcher Henderson](arrangers/1920s-1930s/fletcher-henderson.md) - Father of swing arranging
3. [Duke Ellington](arrangers/1920s-1930s/duke-ellington.md) - Master of orchestral jazz
4. [Benny Carter](arrangers/1920s-1930s/benny-carter.md) - The King (alto sax and arranging)
5. [Sy Oliver](arrangers/1920s-1930s/sy-oliver.md) - Swing era innovator
6. [Mary Lou Williams](arrangers/1920s-1930s/mary-lou-williams.md) - First lady of jazz arranging
7. [Jimmy Mundy](arrangers/1920s-1930s/jimmy-mundy.md) - Benny Goodman's secret weapon
8. [Edgar Sampson](arrangers/1920s-1930s/edgar-sampson.md) - Creator of swing standards
9. [Bill Challis](arrangers/1920s-1930s/bill-challis.md) - Paul Whiteman's arranging genius
10. [Eddie Durham](arrangers/1920s-1930s/eddie-durham.md) - Electric guitar pioneer and arranger
11. [Will Hudson](arrangers/1920s-1930s/will-hudson.md) - Composer-arranger of the swing era
12. [Ferde Grofé](arrangers/1920s-1930s/ferde-grofe.md) - Symphonic jazz pioneer
13. [Glenn Miller](arrangers/1920s-1930s/glenn-miller.md) - Creator of the Miller sound
14. [Horace Henderson](arrangers/1920s-1930s/horace-henderson.md) - Fletcher's younger brother
15. [Andy Kirk](arrangers/1920s-1930s/andy-kirk.md) - Kansas City sound architect
